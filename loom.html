Community Guidelines
Loom by Altura Technologies · Version 2.3 · Last updated 15 March 2016
These guidelines apply to all Loom users regardless of region, jurisdiction, or device. By using Loom you agree to abide by them. Significant changes will be announced via the official Loom account.

1. Our community values

Loom is a platform for genuine community — the kind that existed before the internet, in town squares and community halls and the space between neighbours who actually know each other. We believe that the best communities are self-governing: people who share a place, whether physical or virtual, tend to know what they need and how to look after each other.

We also believe that communities require some rules to function, particularly when they grow. These guidelines exist not to restrict conversation but to protect it — to ensure that the platform remains a place where genuine exchange is possible rather than one where the loudest or most hostile voices crowd everyone else out.

The four values that underpin everything on Loom are:

Kindness
Treat others as neighbours, not as opponents. Disagreement is not only permitted but welcome. Contempt, cruelty, and deliberate humiliation are not.
Honesty
Post in good faith. Don't share information you know or suspect to be false. Don't misrepresent yourself, your affiliations, or your intentions.
Legality
Don't use Loom to facilitate, plan, or encourage illegal activity. Content that violates Halonesian law or the laws of your jurisdiction is not permitted.
Respect for others
Recognise that other people have equal standing in this community. Their presence here is as legitimate as yours. Treat them accordingly.

If you are uncertain whether something is appropriate, ask yourself whether it would be welcome in a real community meeting — a town hall, a neighbourhood association, a local noticeboard. If it wouldn't belong there, it probably doesn't belong here.

2. What's welcome on Loom

Loom is designed for the full range of community life. The following are all explicitly welcome:

📰
Local news and notices
Events, road closures, business openings and closures, local government decisions, neighbourhood changes, weather and infrastructure updates.
🛒
Classifieds and marketplace
Buying, selling, renting, trading, and giving away goods and services within the community. See Section 5 for marketplace-specific rules.
❓
Questions and requests
Practical questions to the community: recommendations, directions, advice, skill-sharing, requests for help with local knowledge.
💬
Debate and discussion
Respectful disagreement on matters of public concern. Political, civic, and community debate is welcomed provided it remains within the conduct rules in Section 3.
⚠️
Alerts and safety information
Emergency notices, safety warnings, missing persons, lost animals, and hazard alerts from credible sources. Alert-category posts receive heightened distribution within the affected region.
🗳️
Polls and community consultation
Open polling on community matters. Poll results are public and non-binding. Polls designed to deceive or manipulate rather than genuinely consult are not permitted.
📅
Events and organising
Community events, meetings, gatherings, and coordination for lawful purposes. Event posts must include accurate date, time, and location information.
🌱
Ordinary community life
Humour, warmth, shared experience, mutual support, and the texture of everyday life in a community. Loom is not only for urgent or civic matters.
3. What isn't permitted

The following categories of content are prohibited on Loom. This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. Content that violates the spirit of these rules will be treated as if it violated the letter of them.

Harassment and targeted abuse

Repeatedly targeting a specific individual or group with hostile, threatening, or degrading messages. Coordinated campaigns to silence, intimidate, or drive someone off the platform. Posting, encouraging others to post, or linking to content intended to humiliate a specific person.

A single heated exchange does not constitute harassment. Sustained, repeated targeting does. Moderators consider the pattern of behaviour across posts and comments, not individual messages in isolation.

Doxxing and privacy violations

Sharing private information about another person without their consent: home addresses, workplaces, personal phone numbers, financial information, medical information, immigration status, or the identity of family members.

This applies regardless of whether the information is technically available from other sources. The act of aggregating and publishing it on Loom with the intent to expose or harm a person is prohibited.

Hate speech and dehumanisation

Content that dehumanises people based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, or other protected characteristics. This includes imagery, language, and rhetoric that portrays groups as subhuman, inherently criminal, or deserving of violence or exclusion.

Criticism of ideas, belief systems, institutions, and policies is permitted and protected. Criticism that slides into dehumanisation of people who hold those beliefs is not. Moderators assess the totality of a post — including framing, language, and context — rather than applying a purely keyword-based test.

Incitement to violence

Explicit or implicit calls for violence against any person, group, institution, or community. This includes content framed as hypothetical, satirical, or rhetorical that a reasonable person would understand as encouraging or celebrating violence. Content glorifying past acts of targeted violence is also prohibited.

Misinformation and deliberate deception

Knowingly false factual claims presented as true, particularly where they could cause real-world harm. This includes fabricated quotes attributed to real people, false emergency alerts, and false reports about ongoing incidents or events.

Opinion and speculation, clearly labelled as such, are not misinformation. Good-faith factual errors are not misinformation. The relevant test is whether the poster knows or should reasonably know that what they are sharing is false. Health misinformation during declared emergencies is addressed separately in Section 6.

Spam and inauthentic behaviour

Repetitive or automated posting designed to flood the feed or manipulate trending topics. Creating multiple accounts to amplify a viewpoint, evade a posting restriction, or simulate broader community support than genuinely exists. Coordinated campaigns that use Loom to manufacture the appearance of organic community sentiment.

Commercial advertising without community relevance. Unsolicited promotion of external businesses or services in non-marketplace posts.

Impersonation

Claiming to be a person or organisation you are not. This includes display names that could plausibly be mistaken for official accounts, public figures, or verified organisations. Satire accounts are permitted only if clearly and unambiguously labelled as satire in the display name or bio.

Impersonating emergency services, healthcare providers, or government bodies is treated as a serious violation regardless of intent.

Illegal content

Content that violates applicable law, including content that facilitates criminal activity, content involving the sexual exploitation of minors, content that violates intellectual property rights, and content that contravenes Halonesia's Emergency Powers Regulations or equivalent territorial law. Loom is not a platform for the organisation or facilitation of unlawful activity in any jurisdiction where our users are located.

4. Sensitive and contested topics

Some topics require particular care not because they are prohibited but because they are genuinely contested, emotionally charged, or prone to generating content that crosses into prohibited territory. Loom does not prohibit discussion of these topics, but applies heightened moderation attention and asks users to take additional care.

Territorial and sovereignty disputes
Discussion of territorial disputes is permitted. Claims about sovereignty, borders, and political status should be clearly framed as position or opinion rather than undisputed fact where they are genuinely contested. Content that calls for violence against populations in disputed territories is prohibited regardless of the poster's political position.
Religious and cultural content
Critique, commentary, and discussion of religious beliefs, practices, and institutions is permitted and protected. Content that moves from critique of belief to dehumanisation of believers is not. The same standard applies to cultural practices and communities.
Mental health and self-harm
Discussion of mental health, grief, and personal crisis is welcome. Content that directly or indirectly encourages, instructs, or glorifies self-harm or suicide is prohibited. If you or someone you know is in crisis, contact emergency services or a crisis line in your territory.
Graphic and disturbing content
Graphic imagery depicting violence, injury, death, or similar content should be accompanied by a content warning. Purely gratuitous graphic content — shared for shock value with no informational purpose — may be removed even where it does not otherwise violate these guidelines. Use the content warning (CW) feature when posting anything that may distress readers.
Electoral and civic processes
Discussion of elections, candidates, and civic processes is permitted. False information about voting procedures, polling locations, eligibility, or official results is prohibited. Content designed to discourage eligible people from participating in lawful civic processes is prohibited.
5. Marketplace rules

The Loom marketplace exists for community members to buy, sell, trade, and give away goods and services locally. The following rules apply to all marketplace and classified listings:

  • Listings must accurately describe the item or service being offered. Deliberate misdescription is prohibited.
  • Price must be clearly stated or clearly indicated as negotiable. Hidden fees revealed only after contact are prohibited.
  • Listings for illegal goods or services are prohibited. This includes controlled substances, unlicensed weapons, stolen goods, and counterfeit items.
  • Live animals may be listed for rehoming but not for sale. Commercial animal sales are not permitted on Loom.
  • Adult services and content are not permitted in any listing category.
  • Listings must be for genuine transactions. Fictitious listings designed to harvest contact information are prohibited.
  • Once a transaction is complete, mark the item as sold. Leaving completed listings active wastes community members' time.
  • Loom does not mediate disputes between buyers and sellers. All transactions are between private parties.
Loom accepts no liability for transactions conducted through the platform. Buyers and sellers transact at their own risk. Exercise appropriate caution, particularly with high-value items or when meeting in person.

6. Public health emergency rules

During a declared public health emergency — including the current emergency in effect as of 01 May 2016 — additional content rules apply to all health-related posts on Loom, regardless of region. These rules are in effect until the relevant emergency is formally lifted by the Halonesian Health and Safety Council or the competent territorial authority.

The following are prohibited during a declared emergency:

✗
Claims about transmission routes, symptoms, or disease progression that directly contradict current guidance from the Halonesia Health and Safety Council or recognised territorial health authorities.
✗
Mortality rate or case count figures that significantly deviate from official reported figures without credible sourcing from a recognised medical institution or peer-reviewed publication.
✗
Promotion of unproven, unapproved, or counterfeit treatments, cures, or preventative measures, including herbal, alternative, or traditional remedies presented as effective against the declared pathogen.
✗
Claims that the disease is a deliberate biological attack, a manufactured crisis, or the result of covert government or corporate activity, unless supported by credible evidence from a verified journalistic or institutional source.
✗
Ethnic, racial, or national scapegoating in connection with any outbreak — attributing disease spread to particular communities, peoples, or nationalities.
✗
Advice that encourages evasion of lawfully imposed quarantine, movement restrictions, or contact tracing requirements. This includes advice about routes, timings, or methods of travel that circumvent active cordon controls.
✗
Content designed to interfere with or obstruct official emergency response operations, including the operations of designated response contractors operating under government authority.

Posts violating these rules will be removed without warning. Repeat violations during a declared emergency will result in immediate suspension pending review, without the graduated response that applies to other violations.

Accurate, up-to-date health information is available through verified official accounts on Loom. Look for the ✓ verified indicator on posts from health authorities.

These rules reflect obligations on Loom under the Halonesia Emergency Information Management Act 2015 and do not represent a departure from Loom's general commitment to open community discussion. During declared emergencies, the health and safety of community members takes precedence over the full scope of expression normally permitted on the platform.

7. Regional and political content

Loom covers multiple territories with distinct political situations, some of which are actively contested. The following rules apply to regionally and politically sensitive content:

  • Factual reporting on regional political situations is permitted. Claims should be made in good faith and, where contested, labelled as opinion or perspective rather than established fact.
  • Content calling for violence against the population of any territory, region, or political unit is prohibited absolutely and without exception.
  • Loom recognises the self-governing status of formally recognised territorial entities. Disputing that status is permitted as a matter of political opinion but must be framed as opinion.
  • Content produced by or on behalf of a government, political party, or campaign without clear disclosure of that fact is treated as inauthentic behaviour and subject to removal.
  • Users may post about political matters in regions other than their own. Regional filters are not a moderation tool — they are an organisational tool.
  • Content originating from outside Halonesia is not exempt from these guidelines. Loom is a global platform and these rules apply to all users regardless of location.
8. Anonymity and identity

Loom permits anonymous posting as a feature. Anonymity has genuine value — it allows people to discuss sensitive personal matters, report community issues without fear of retaliation, and participate in civic life where identification might carry risk.

However, anonymity does not exempt users from these guidelines. Anonymous posts are subject to the same rules as identified posts. When a post is submitted, Loom stores the submitting user's identity token alongside it regardless of whether the post is displayed as anonymous. This information may be accessed in the context of a moderation investigation or a lawful authority request.

What anonymous means on Loom
  • Your display name is not shown on the post. "Anonymous" is shown instead.
  • Your avatar colour is not shown. A neutral colour is used.
  • Your identity token is still recorded in the platform's data store.
  • Your identity can be determined by Loom administrators during moderation review.
  • Your identity may be disclosed to authorities in response to a lawful request.

Misuse of anonymous posting to circumvent moderation actions — posting anonymously after a named account has been restricted — is treated as a violation equivalent to the original violation.

9. How moderation works

Loom does not pre-screen content before it is published. Content is moderated reactively — in response to reports, automated flag triggers, or proactive review by moderators during declared emergencies or elevated risk periods.

Standard review
Reported posts are reviewed by a human moderator within 24 hours. Moderators assess reports against these guidelines. A report does not automatically result in removal — moderators make independent judgements. Misusing the report function to silence legitimate speech is itself a violation.
Emergency review
During a declared public health emergency, health-related reports are escalated to a four-hour review target. Posts that are clearly and unambiguously in violation of Section 6 may be removed without individual review during active emergency periods.
Administrator actions
Regional and platform administrators may pin, remove, or lock posts. All such actions are logged internally. Administrators are bound by these guidelines in the exercise of their powers and may not remove posts solely on the basis of disagreement with the expressed viewpoint.
Automated detection
Loom uses automated tools to detect potential violations of these guidelines. Automated flags are reviewed by a human moderator before action is taken in all cases except where emergency removal authority applies.
10. Reporting and appeals

Use the Report button beneath any post to flag it for moderation review. Reports are anonymous — the person whose post you are reporting will not be notified that a report was made. You will not receive notification of the outcome of a report unless you are the subject of the action taken.

If your post is removed, you will be informed via a notice on your next session. The notice will identify which guideline was cited and whether the removal is subject to appeal. Not all removals are appealable — posts removed under emergency health provisions during a declared emergency are not subject to appeal during the emergency period.

Misusing the report function — reporting posts purely to silence a viewpoint you disagree with, making false or bad-faith reports, or coordinating with others to mass-report a user — is itself a violation of these guidelines and may result in your reporting privileges being suspended.

Appeals are handled through the Altura Trust and Safety team via the contact link in the Loom footer. Appeals must be submitted within 14 days of the removal notice. Appeals submitted outside this window will not be reviewed. Allow 5 working days for appeal review.
11. Altura's role and data practices

Loom is a product of Altura Technologies. Understanding the relationship between Loom and Altura is important for informed use of the platform.

Your posts are public
Everything you post on Loom — including posts to specific regional feeds — is visible to all Loom users and stored on the Gun.js peer-to-peer network. There is no private posting on Loom (other than direct messages, addressed below). Do not post information on Loom that you would not be comfortable having widely read.
Your identity is stored locally
Display names and identity tokens are generated on your device and stored in your browser's local storage. Altura does not maintain a centralised account database linking display names to personal identities. If you clear your browser data, your identity token is lost and cannot be recovered. Anonymous posting stores your identity token alongside the post on the network regardless of display.
Usage data is collected and retained
Altura collects and retains data about how you use Loom. This includes: session information (when you use the platform, for how long, and from what network address), content interaction data (which posts you view, like, comment on, or report), posting behaviour (frequency, category, region, timing, and hashtag usage), and device and browser characteristics. This data is retained and associated with your identity token for the period of your use of the platform and thereafter as permitted by applicable law.
The Insights feature
The Insights widget in the sidebar shows you a portion of the data Altura Analytics holds about your activity on Loom: your post count, engagement statistics, peak activity times, and top hashtags. This data exists and is retained by Altura regardless of whether you choose to view it in the Insights panel. The panel is a transparency feature, not a data-creation feature.
Cross-product data integration
If you use other Altura products — including Waypoint (mapping), Herald (news), Vault (payments), WellCheck (health tracking), Scout (employment), or others — data from those products may be associated with your Loom usage by Altura Analytics for internal research, product development, and analytics purposes. This association is performed using device identifiers and network characteristics, not personal identification data. You cannot opt out of cross-product association while using Altura products.
Direct messages
Direct messages on Loom are transmitted peer-to-peer using Gun.js and are not stored on Altura's servers. They exist only while both parties are connected. Altura does not have access to the content of direct messages. However, metadata associated with DM sessions — the fact that a session occurred, its timing, and the identity tokens of the parties — may be accessible from network logs.
Disclosure to authorities
In compliance with applicable law, Altura may provide content, identity tokens, and associated metadata to law enforcement, government bodies, or other authorities in response to a lawful request. Altura is subject to the disclosure obligations of the Halonesia Data Security and Access Act 2014 and to the terms of its agreements with the Altura Internal Security Directorate. During a declared public health emergency or equivalent crisis, the threshold for lawful disclosure requests is reduced under the Emergency Information Management Act 2015. Altura may not always be able to notify users when their data has been disclosed.
Full details of Altura's data practices are in the Altura Privacy Policy, available at altura.gov.hl/privacy. The Altura Privacy Policy governs in the event of any conflict between it and this section of the Community Guidelines.
12. Consequences

Consequences for violations are applied at moderator discretion and may vary based on severity, intent, pattern, and context. The following table describes the standard graduated response. Loom reserves the right to depart from this framework where the nature of the violation warrants it.

Level
Response
First minor
violation
Post removal and written notice. No further action. Moderator may add a note to the account record. No posting restriction applied.
Repeated minor
violations
Post removal and temporary posting restriction (24–72 hours). User is notified of the restriction and its duration. Further violations during the restriction period extend the restriction.
Serious
violation
Immediate post removal and account suspension pending review. Applies to: targeted harassment, doxxing, hate speech, incitement, health misinformation during declared emergency, impersonation of emergency services. Review target: 48 hours.
Severe
violation
Immediate removal and extended suspension. Referral to Altura Trust and Safety for review. May include referral to relevant authorities where legally required. Applies to: coordinated inauthentic behaviour at scale, repeated serious violations, wilful and systematic emergency rule violations.
Permanent
removal
Applied for: incitement to violence, content involving the sexual exploitation of minors, systematic wilful violation of emergency health rules, or any conduct Altura determines makes continued access to the platform incompatible with community safety. Permanent removal is not subject to appeal.

Because Loom uses device-local identity rather than registered accounts, conventional account suspension is implemented through platform-level bans on the identity token. Loom cannot prevent a determined user from generating a new identity token. Circumventing a suspension by generating a new identity is itself a violation and will result in escalation to the next level of consequence.

Loom may share information about suspended identity tokens with Altura Analytics and, where legally required, with relevant authorities.

These guidelines apply to all Loom users regardless of region or jurisdiction. Altura reserves the right to update them at any time. Significant updates will be announced via the official Loom account.
